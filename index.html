Contribute meaningfully as civil servant, as an employee, and to the lives of people I care most about.

In 2013 I received an amazing opportunity to join the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) as a Research Fellow. I packed up and moved from Chicago (really cold winters) to Atlanta (really hot summers). Three years later I was accepted into the CDC Public Health Informatics Fellowship.

In this role, I am committed to arming public health programs with the infrastructure and information they need to improve the effectiveness of their services and continue to seek to understand different frameworks and best practice that will help identify, analyze, and visualize information that can influence change and effectively communicate work to others.
